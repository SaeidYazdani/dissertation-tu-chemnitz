\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:ch-vdut-introduction}

As established in \autoref{ch:casestudy-from-ate-to-dut}, power supply and
signal integrity in an \ac{ATE} environment depends on multiple
quality factors:

\begin{itemize}[topsep=8pt,itemsep=4pt,partopsep=4pt, parsep=4pt]
    \item Power supply (control loop speed, compensation, etc.)
    \item Interconnects between \ac{ATE} and \ac{DUT}
    \item Load board and test socket
    \item Internal layout and structure of \ac{DUT} (chip design)
    \item Test program (timing, start-up sequence, etc.)
\end{itemize}

Verifying behaviour of an \ac{ATE} system as a whole proves to be
challenging, as it is not trivial to measure exactly what is being received
on \ac{DUT} pins just by probing other points that may ignore the properties
of downstream or upstream in the \ac{ATE} to \ac{DUT} path.

One solution is to use a special \ac{DUT} that sits inside
the socket and stimulates the total setup and captures the behavior of
the \ac{ATE} setup with \emph{control} and waveform \emph{capture} capabilities.

Such a solution needs to be similar to the \ac{DUT} in terms of electrical
properties and physical appearance as well as matching the \ac{DUT} in
footprint and dimensions.
